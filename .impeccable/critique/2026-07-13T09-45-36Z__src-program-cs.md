## Anti-Patterns Verdict

The editor foundation is credible, but the landing page reads as template-driven AI UI: a violet-to-blue synthetic S mark, centered hero, oversized rounded drop card, tiny uppercase eyebrow text, and multiple decorative accent colors. The real UX Live brand is reduced to a small footer mark.

Manual static evidence on commit `4de7b98` found 26 `Glyph*` references, 20 font sites below 9 pt, 13 uppercase English UI-string sites, 21 mixed-accent references, 235 manual scale calls, no explicit accessible names or roles, and 38 mixed decorative separators.

## Overall Impression

The flow is fundamentally sound and the editor already feels like a creator tool. The biggest opportunity is to replace generic visual styling with an unmistakable UX Live system while keeping import, edit, and export continuously visible at normal Windows DPI.

## What's Working

- Drag and drop gives the first screen one obvious entry point.
- Preview, timeline, crop, and background removal are grouped into a sensible production flow.
- Dark editing surfaces suit video work and keep colorful footage visually dominant.

## Priority Issues

### P0: Window and key actions are clipped at normal DPI

The 1080 × 720 client size is manually scaled again, producing a workspace larger than the available screen and hiding the inspector/export action. Size the form from `Screen.WorkingArea`, keep form chrome in physical pixels, and progressively hide secondary canvas metadata before primary actions.

### P1: Brand and icon system feel generic

Two synthetic gradient S marks and private-use MDL2 glyphs make the product look assembled rather than designed. Use the bundled UX Live logo everywhere, derive a single brand accent from it, and replace font glyphs with one consistent native vector family.

### P1: The primary result is not persistent

Users can lose sight of export and its blocking reason. Keep the export control visible in the readiness inspector with a precise disabled label and a compact checklist.

### P1: Typography and localization are too small and inconsistent

Twenty source sites use fonts below 9 pt and the UI mixes `TOOLS`, `CANVAS`, `CREATIVE WORKSPACE`, Russian labels, and three separator styles. Raise visible metadata to at least 9 pt, use Russian task language, and simplify separators.

### P1: Custom controls lack strong keyboard and accessibility semantics

The drop area is mouse-first, UI controls do not expose explicit accessible names/roles, and global Space can override a focused control. Add keyboard activation, visible 2 px focus, accessible roles/names, Esc exits, and accelerators for open/crop/background/export.

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User):** Crop, background removal, and export require the mouse; export can leave the viewport; only undo and playback expose accelerators.

**Jordan (First-Timer):** Import is obvious, but after load the sequence from crop requirement to a valid WebM is not visually continuous. English workspace labels and the term “кроп” add translation work.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent):** Tiny low-contrast metadata, custom glyph controls, missing UI Automation semantics, and a clipped viewport make low-vision and keyboard-only use risky.

## Minor Observations

- Status pills resemble action buttons and are over-rounded.
- The footer UX Live lockup competes with the privacy line.
- The canvas grid is appropriate, but should remain quieter than the footage.
- Motion should stay minimal in this high-frequency editor; immediate pressed feedback is more valuable than decorative transitions.
